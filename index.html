Travel style summary

This plan is built for an adventure couple trip: heights are okay, hiking should not be too intense, no elephant sanctuary, no safari park, and no car rental. The biggest planning rule is luggage: big bags stay at the hotel before waterfall/rafting activities, then you return to shower/change before checkout.

🎯
Best overall optionOption A gives the cleanest balance: Erawan night first, bag-safe waterfall morning, then adventure park + cave + rafting day, final night near station.
🧳
Bag ruleNever carry big bags to Erawan or onto river activity. Use hotel luggage storage and return for shower/change before checkout.
🚕
Transport realityTree Top officially has no transfer service, so any plan including it should use one paid driver/transfer day.
🌧️
May weatherMay can have hot weather and sudden rain. For river activity, confirm same-day water/current/weather conditions before paying.

Main places included

Adventure core

Tree Top Adventure Park

Home Phutoey River Kwai Resort, Sai Yok

~฿1,000 / person promo

  • Closest match to Adrenalin Forest Wellington: high ropes, ziplines, rope bridges, Tarzan swing-style elements
  • 40 challenges, about 1.5–2 hours
  • Best visit: 09:00 or 11:00 session, booked in advance
  • No transfer service from official page, so plan paid driver/transfer
Must include

Erawan Waterfall

Seven-tier waterfall, best swimming highlight

฿60 Thai / ฿300 foreign adult entry

  • Main nature/swimming day; bring swimsuit, towel, dry bag, sandals with grip
  • Bag rule: keep big bags at hotel, return to shower/change before checkout
  • Do not force all 7 levels; focus on swimming pools and lower-to-middle levels
  • Leave before late afternoon because last buses can be unreliable
Added

ล่องแก่ง / river activity

Guided bamboo raft, kayak, or river float

~฿400–900 / person est.

  • Use guided provider only with life jacket and staff support
  • Best in Sai Yok/River Kwai Noi corridor or provider near your stay
  • Book/confirm same day conditions because river current depends on weather/dam release
  • Bring dry bag; do not bring passports/laptops to water activity
